Day 1 · Practical

Ghent Survival Guide

🚗

The Low Emission Zone (LEZ)

If you're coming by car, you need to register your license plate BEFORE you drive into the city. Otherwise you'll get an automatic fine — and that's not the best way to start your internship.

How to register: Go to stad.gent/lez — takes 5 minutes. Do it before your first day, not after.
🏠

Housing

Start looking early. Really early. Ghent fills up fast, especially for short-term rentals. Waiting until the last minute means you'll end up on Airbnb paying way too much.

Good platforms: ColocHousing, Wing Upkot, ikot.be — start there before anything else.
🍺

After Work

Ghent is a great city. Good food, good bars, good people. The old town is worth exploring. And if you like Belgian beer — you are in the right place.

Tip: Don't just go home after work. The city is part of the experience — enjoy it.
🚲

Getting Around

Ghent is very bike-friendly. Most people cycle everywhere. Public transport works well too. The city center is compact — you won't need a car for day-to-day life once you're set up.

Blue-bike shared bikes are available everywhere — cheap and convenient for the first days.

Day 2 · The honest stuff

Real Talk

1

Take notes from day one

You'll forget everything otherwise. The product, the names, the processes — write it all down. There's no shame in it, there's only efficiency.

2

Ask questions early

If you're unsure about a task, just ask. Don't wait until you've spent 3 hours going in the wrong direction. The team is approachable — use that.

3

Your work is visible

In a team this size, what you do gets noticed — good and bad. Show up, give your best, and take ownership of your projects from start to finish.

4

Use uman yourself

Don't just read about the product — use it. The best way to understand what you're working on is to actually experience it like a sales rep would.

5

Think about the company

Not just your task. Every decision should be: is this good for uman? That mindset is what separates a good intern from a great one here.

6

Introduce yourself to everyone

Don't stick only to the people you're assigned to. In a small team, knowing everyone makes everything easier — and more fun.

★

Sam is your main point of reference

He oversees the commercial side and assigned this internship. If you're unsure about your direction or priorities, go see him — don't wait until the end of the week.
